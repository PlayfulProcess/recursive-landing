## Tone & Voice Guidelines

Based on your Substack writing, here are specific voice principles:

### ✅ Do:
- **Use "I" statements**: "I'm learning..." not "We believe..."
- **Acknowledge uncertainty**: "I'm still exploring..." builds trust
- **Share process**: Show the messy middle, not just polished outcomes
- **Use metaphor grounded in experience**: Spiral, dance, Siddhartha—not abstract concepts
- **Ask open questions**: Invite reflection without demanding answers
- **Reference your influences**: Gottman, Tillich, Andreotti—shows intellectual humility

### ❌ Don't:
- **Promise outcomes**: No "I'll help you find peace" or "achieve wholeness"
- **Use therapeutic jargon without translation**: Explain "secure attachment," don't assume
- **Sound like a guru**: You're a fellow traveler, not an enlightened master
- **Separate categories artificially**: Tools/therapy/philosophy are integrated, not siloed
- **Use urgency tactics**: No "limited spots" or "don't miss out"

## Measuring Success: Slow Metrics

Forget vanity metrics. For a therapy practice waitlist, track:

### Quantitative:
- **Email signups:** Target 50-100/year = 150-300 by launch
  - With 10-20% conversion to actual clients = 15-60 clients
  - At 20-30 clients for full practice, you're well-positioned
- **Email open rates:** 40%+ means you're maintaining trust

### Qualitative (More Important):
- **Email replies to quarterly updates:** Even 2-3 thoughtful responses mean you're connecting
- **Substack comments:** Depth > quantity. One "This is exactly what I needed" > 100 likes
- **Referrals:** "My friend might resonate with your work" = highest trust signal

### The Real Metric:
When you're ready to take clients in 2026-2027, do you have 20-30 people who:
1. Remember who you are
2. Trust your approach

If yes, you've succeeded. Everything else is noise.

## Final Thoughts: The Humble Invitation

You wrote: "I don't want to sound as if I am selling myself. I want to sound very humble and grounded, and yet, available."

This is the hardest tone to strike because our culture only teaches two modes:
1. **Aggressive sales:** "Transform your life! Limited spots!"
2. **False humility:** "I'm just here, maybe I can help, no pressure..."

**You need a third way: Grounded availability.**

### Grounded availability sounds like:

✅ "I'm preparing to offer process-oriented therapy in 2026-2027. If you're interested, join the waiting list."
- Clear timeline
- No urgency
- Simple invitation

✅ "I'm not offering techniques to fix you. I'm offering space to explore what it means to live well with mystery."
- Honest about what you do/don't offer
- Confident in your approach
- Humble about what's possible

✅ "This is an invitation, not a commitment. You can unsubscribe anytime."
- Respects autonomy
- Reduces pressure
- Trust-building

### What to avoid:

❌ "I can help you find peace/purpose/wholeness"
- Promises outcomes you can't control

❌ "Join my community of seekers"
- Sounds cultish, not clinical

❌ "Only a few spots available"
- Creates false scarcity
- Contradicts slow-build approach
