What Are Recursive Patterns?

Recursive patterns emerge when simple rules or elements repeat and combine with themselves, creating increasingly complex and beautiful structures. Like fractals in nature, each level of the pattern contains the seeds of the whole.

They represent a fundamental principle of how complexity emerges from simplicity - how meaning-making itself might work recursively, with each understanding building upon and transforming previous ones.

Inspiration: Fernando Carnauba

Fernando Carnauba's Combinatorics series explores how unit squares can be assembled into countless variations. His work demonstrates that "in paintings with 96 pieces, the number of possible arrangements surpasses the number of atoms in our known universe."

This is the art I'm trying to reconstruct interactively - exploring how simple modular units, through systematic yet creative variation, can generate patterns of extraordinary complexity and beauty.

The Challenge: "Orientation in thinking" (Kant's term) is hard. How do we navigate the infinite space of possibilities to find meaningful patterns? This is both an artistic and philosophical question.
