The Question

Can we create conditions where virtuous meaning emerges not as something we do, but as something the system itself generates and regenerates?

I have an underlying hypothesis that recursiveness becomes regeneration when it is achieved through virtuous meaning-making. If you think about it, recursiveness is just a how. Just like infrastructure is just a what. If directed toward passions, they might create chaos. But if directed towards virtue, they might create cosmos, even if in a virtual reality. We need to start practicing somewhere.

For recursiveness itself to spiral to order and not to chaos, it needs to be virtuous. So, if we can create a make belief world in which we are recursively creating meaning endlessly, it has to be virtuous. Virtue actually gets out of a theoretical framework and becomes a feature. If bugged, the recursiveness will stop. If embedded, maybe it will spread even outside its make believed original reality into the world out there.

The Potential

If done right, the potential is huge. Virtue is pretty scalable. Unlike material resources that diminish when shared, virtue multiplies through infinite synthesis—what you share, you gain—creating expanding cycles of mutual transformation that strengthen through participation.

Recursive Models in Action

Recursiveness isn't just theory—it's how many sustainable organizations thrive. Here are examples of recursive business models where giving freely creates value that sustains itself:

Understanding Recursive Processes

Recursion means the process improves itself through use. Each cycle creates better conditions for the next cycle.

Multi-rhythm recursive process

The Recursive Cycle

1
Share Freely

Create valuable tools, content, or services available to everyone

2
Community Builds

Free access attracts users who find value and form community

3
Value Creates Sustainability

Community members support through donations, premium services, or contributions

4
Resources Enable Sharing

Sustainable funding improves tools, attracts more users, strengthening the cycle

The Magic: Each cycle produces better tools and stronger community, creating conditions for even more generous sharing. The system becomes more capable through use.

Successful Recursive Models

👻 Ghost Model

Ghost demonstrates recursive business perfectly: their open-source software is free, which attracts users. Many users want hassle-free hosting, so they pay for Ghost(Pro). That revenue funds the nonprofit Ghost Foundation, improving the software, attracting more users, creating more Ghost(Pro) demand—a virtuous cycle.

"The more people use Ghost, the more demand there is for our paid service, the more revenue we make, the better the software gets, the more people use Ghost... and so on. It's a virtuous cycle."

🛠️ Open Source + Premium

Platforms like Vercel & Supabase give away powerful tools freely, building massive developer communities. Developers learn, build projects, and eventually need premium features for production. The free tier creates the very users who fuel the paid tier, while paid customers fund continued free access—recursive sustainability.

The Pattern: Free access builds community → Community creates demand for premium → Premium revenue sustains free access → Cycle continues

👨‍💻 Developer Consulting

Most successful open source developers, including Jon O'Nolan before Ghost, made their living contributing to free software while earning through consulting, custom implementations, and support services. This model lets developers build what they're passionate about while getting paid for their expertise—the free software demonstrates their skills and attracts clients.

The Cycle: Open source work builds reputation → Reputation attracts consulting clients → Client work funds more open source → Better software attracts more clients

Free Software & Recursive Publics

We are joining the existing Free Software movement through an experiment in recursive public technology—where writing, reading, and sharing become acts of collective meaning-making. Our journaling tools invite you to reflect and discover meaning through writing. Our channels create spaces to read and learn from others' wisdom. Our studies inspire you to create art and understanding.

Kelty defined the Free Software movement as a Recursive public, that is "a public that is vitally concerned with the material and practical maintenance and modification of the technical, legal, practical, and conceptual means of its own existence as a public; it is a collective independent of other forms of constituted power and is capable of speaking to existing forms of power through the production of actually existing alternatives."

—Christopher Kelty in Two Bits

We will try to operate in a way to contribute to this virtuous "recursiveness."
