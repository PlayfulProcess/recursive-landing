What "Recursive" Means Here

"Recursive" sounds technical, but the idea is simple:

We change the tools, and the tools change us.
We make culture, and culture makes us.

On Recursive.eco, recursiveness means looping through this process on purpose:

1
Receive
We take in the world: stories, tools, code, culture, other people's work.
2
Reflect
We write, journal, talk, pay attention to what it does to us.
3
Create
We make something from that‚Äîplaylists, kids' stories, tools, practices.
4
Share
We offer it back to others, under open licenses, so they can adapt and improve it.
5
Repeat, slowly
Each loop hopefully becomes a little more virtuous: kinder, wiser, safer.

That's what "recursive" is here:
meaning that spirals, rather than scrolls; loops of giving and receiving that make life a bit more livable.

Why I Built This (The Mati & Dada Moment)

A while back, my daughter was watching Mati & Dada‚Äîa gentle art-history cartoon. On their YouTube channel, there was a link to the old official website.

One click away from that beloved kids' show‚Ä¶ was soft porn.

The domain had been hijacked after being abandoned. Technically, it was "just bots repurposing an old URL." But as a parent, the mechanism doesn't matter. The risk does.

Around the same time, my own old domain was quietly turned into gambling links. I realized: the web forgets what things were "meant" to be. Links decay. Algorithms don't care. Children are not the intended audience of this infrastructure.

So I built a small thing:

Creator.Recursive.Eco ‚Äî a tool to watch YouTube in a calmer way:
no thumbnails, no algorithm, no rabbit holes. Just curated sequences you control.

You can:

  • Discover curated playlists and kids' stories
  • Make your own mixed sequences (videos, images, text)
  • Share them with others, if you want

It doesn't replace supervision, and it's still just a browser. But it creates a safer middle ground between "full YouTube" and "no screen at all."

That was the first loop of recursive gratitude: something scary happened, I learned from it, and then tried to give something back.

Why It's Open Source & "Recursive"

I'm deeply influenced by the Free Software movement, especially Christopher Kelty's idea of a "recursive public":

A public that cares not only about sharing ideas,
but about maintaining and modifying the tools and infrastructure that let it exist at all.

In practice, that means:

  • The platform code and content structure are open and forkable.
  • You can copy this site, host your own version, and adapt it.
  • Improvements don't just benefit "me"‚Äîthey can benefit everyone downstream.

I'm inspired by recursive models like:

  • Ghost ‚Äì open-source software, funded by paid hosting, that keeps the code free.
  • Vercel & Supabase ‚Äì generous free tiers that let small experiments (like this one) exist at all.
  • Open source developers & consultants ‚Äì people who give away tools, then make a living helping others use them well.

Recursive.eco is my small translation of that pattern into the worlds of:

  • kids & parents,
  • mental health & meaning, and
  • virtue as a feature, not just a theory.
