Governance Framework

How we organize ourselves as a recursive public - transparent, participatory, and committed to virtuous meaning-making.

We are joining the existing Free Software movement, not creating something entirely new but rather giving it our own flavor and purpose by engaging in conceiving open systems and coordinating collaboration (see below).

Kelty defined the Free Software movement as a Recursive public, that is "a public that is vitally concerned with the material and practical maintenance and modification of the technical, legal, practical, and conceptual means of its own existence as a public; it is a collective independent of other forms of constituted power and is capable of speaking to existing forms of power through the production of actually existing alternatives." ‚ÄîChristopher Kelty in Two Bits. We will try to operate in a way to contribute to this virtuous "recursiveness".

Our Working Hypothesis for Empathetic Moderation

Every action stems from someone trying to meet a universal human need‚Äîconnection, autonomy, safety, contribution, meaning. Even harmful actions are tragic attempts to meet real needs through strategies that don't work.

When needs conflict, we don't choose sides. We look for ways to honor the underlying needs of everyone involved. Sometimes this means creative solutions that meet everyone's needs. Sometimes it means acknowledging that certain strategies for meeting needs cause harm to others, and gently redirecting toward strategies that work.

Our Infrastructure Helps People:

  • Recognize their own needs and the needs of others
  • Express those needs without blame or demand
  • Collaborate on strategies that meet multiple needs at once
  • Modify our systems when they're not serving everyone's needs

When Strategies Cause Harm:

  • First assume good intent and offer support
  • Use clear communication to help translate between different expressions
  • Provide clear feedback about strategies that support the commons
  • Limit access when needed, while keeping the door open for return

This hypothesis itself can be modified by the community. If it's not serving everyone's needs, we'll evolve it together. The goal isn't perfection‚Äîit's learning. We're experimenting with infrastructure that helps humans cultivate virtue together, even when we disagree.
