## Creating Your Story

### Step 1: Find Your Message (15m)

**What do you want your kiddo to know?**

Think about the message you want to share:
- Big feelings are okay
- You belong just as you are
- We can handle hard things together
- Your quiet needs matter too

**Involve your child:**

Let your child help shape the story:
- What's their favorite animal or character type?
- What colors do they love?
- What special powers should the character have?
- What situations are they facing right now?

üåü **Why this matters:** Stories that reflect your child's world resonate deeper and create more meaningful learning moments.

### Step 2: Write Messy & Free (20m)

**Grab paper (or Kindle Scribe) and just GO**

**Writing guidelines:**
- Don't worry about spelling or structure
- Include real situations your child faces
- Let your authentic parent voice shine through
- Make it as messy as you need!

**What to include:**
- The main character (based on your child's input)
- The challenge or situation
- How the character handles it
- What they learn
- How it ends positively

üåü **Why handwritten first?** Your genuine emotions come through better than typing. Kids can feel the difference.

### Step 3: Let AI Work Its Magic (60-90m)

Both [Claude](https://claude.ai) and [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com) work great for this process! They perform fairly equally well, though I personally prefer Claude for OCR (reading handwriting) and ChatGPT for story structuring. Feel free to use whichever you're more comfortable with.

**Transcribe Your Handwriting (OCR)**

**My preference:** Claude - but both work!

- Take photos of your messy pages with your phone
- Upload them to Claude or ChatGPT
- Ask the AI to transcribe your handwriting
- Request suggestions without changing YOUR style

**Example prompt:**
> Please transcribe this handwritten story. Keep my authentic voice and emotional tone intact. After transcribing, suggest improvements for clarity while maintaining my style.

**Structure Your Story**

**My preference:** ChatGPT - but both work!

- Copy your transcribed text
- Paste it into ChatGPT or Claude
- Ask the AI to structure it into book pages
- Specify your child's age for appropriate language

**Example prompt:**
> Structure this story into a children's book with 10-12 pages. My child is [age] years old. Break it into appropriate page lengths with smooth flow and pacing. Give me options to choose from.

**Create Illustrations with DALLE**

**Available in:** ChatGPT (integrated)

- Create detailed prompts for each page
- Request consistent character style throughout
- Include your child's preferences (colors, style, etc.)

**Example prompt:**
> Create an illustration for page 1 of my children's book. Style: soft watercolor illustration. Character: [describe main character with your child's chosen features]. Scene: [describe what's happening]. Use warm, inviting colors with [child's favorite color] prominently featured. Keep the character design consistent for future illustrations.

**Tips for consistent illustrations:**
- Describe the character in detail in your first prompt
- Reference "keep character consistent with previous image" in subsequent prompts
- Save the character description to reuse
- Request the same art style for all illustrations

## Troubleshooting

**Claude can't read my handwriting?**
- Try better lighting when taking photos
- Write a bit larger and clearer
- Upload individual pages rather than multiple at once
- Type the parts Claude struggles with

**ChatGPT changed my voice too much?**
- Be explicit: "Keep my authentic voice and style"
- Ask for options and choose the one closest to your intent
- Edit the output to bring back your voice
- Start with smaller chunks to maintain control

**DALLE images aren't consistent?**
- Save your character description and reuse it exactly
- Reference previous images in your prompts
- Use specific art style descriptions
- Consider generating all at once with same base prompt

**Story feels too long/short?**
- Ask ChatGPT to adjust page count
- Add or remove scenes before structuring
- Target 10-15 pages for most age groups
- Test the length by reading aloud
