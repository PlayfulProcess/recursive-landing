Community Governance Framework

The theoretical foundation behind our experimental governance system.

Our Governance Philosophy

We transform the collective by transforming ourselves. Unlike pure democracy (majority rule) or autocracy (single authority), our governance system evaluates decisions based on Recursive.eco's core principles. Community input guides decision-making, but choices align with our foundational values of individual transformation serving collective evolution‚Äîa principled approach that maintains coherent direction while remaining responsive to community needs.

Building Infrastructure for Recursive Kindness

The Experiment: Can we create conditions where kindness emerges not as something we do, but as something the system itself generates and regenerates?

Key principle: We'll only build what we actually need when we need it. This prevents over-engineering and keeps maintenance manageable for a solo developer.

Framework: Free Software, as outlined in Two Bits, by Christopher Kelty, defined by six principles:

Each person who uses tools to become kinder contributes back kinder tools. Each modification makes the system more capable of fostering kindness. Infrastructure doesn't just enable kindness‚Äîit evolves to become kinder.

Our Working Hypothesis

Every action stems from someone trying to meet a universal human need‚Äîconnection, autonomy, safety, contribution, meaning. Even harmful actions are tragic attempts to meet real needs through strategies that don't work.

When needs conflict, we don't choose sides. We look for ways to honor the underlying needs of everyone involved. Sometimes this means creative solutions that meet everyone's needs. Sometimes it means acknowledging that certain strategies for meeting needs cause harm to others, and gently redirecting toward strategies that work.

Our Infrastructure Helps People:
  • ‚Ä¢ Recognize their own needs and the needs of others
  • ‚Ä¢ Express those needs without blame or demand
  • ‚Ä¢ Collaborate on strategies that meet multiple needs at once
  • ‚Ä¢ Modify our systems when they're not serving everyone's needs
When Strategies Cause Harm:
  • ‚Ä¢ First assume good intent and offer support
  • ‚Ä¢ Use clear communication to help translate between different expressions
  • ‚Ä¢ Provide clear feedback about strategies that support the commons
  • ‚Ä¢ Limit access when needed, while keeping the door open for return

This hypothesis itself can be modified by the community. If it's not serving everyone's needs, we'll evolve it together. The goal isn't perfection‚Äîit's learning. We're experimenting with infrastructure that helps humans be kind to each other, even when we disagree.
