Community Governance Framework

The theoretical foundation behind our experimental governance system.

Building Infrastructure for Meaning-Making Through Public Goods

The Experiment: Can we create conditions where virtue emerges not as something we do, but as something the system itself generates and regenerates?

Key principle: We'll only build what we actually need when we need it. This prevents over-engineering and keeps maintenance manageable for a solo developer.

Framework: Free Software, as outlined in Two Bits, by Christopher Kelty, defined by six principles:

Creating Movement

We are joining the existing Free Software movement, not creating something entirely new. We're experimenting with how virtue-focused infrastructure can contribute to this broader movement by arguing about practices while practicing them‚Äîbuilding spaces for discussing what virtue means in digital spaces through Discourse forums, blog posts, and feedback loops.

Sharing Source Code

Making virtue tools freely available. Everything open source under Creative Commons. Tools work without login or tracking. The means of cultivating virtue can be inspected and modified by anyone.

Conceiving Open Systems

Human-defined values with community execution. Voluntary contribution model. Rules prevent individual abuse while enabling collective flourishing. Discourages platform dependence.

Writing Copyright Licenses

Creative Commons BY-SA creates genealogy of virtue practices. ShareAlike ensures improvements benefit everyone. Legal framework prevents virtue from being enclosed or commodified.

Coordinating Collaborations

Recursive governance where the virtue system modifies itself. Community-defined guidelines and transparent hierarchies with modifiable rules. Harmonizing tensions: Safety-Freedom, Care-Adventure, Individual-Collective.

The Recursive Principle

Each person who uses tools to become more virtuous contributes back more virtuous tools. Each modification makes the system more capable of fostering virtue. Infrastructure doesn't just enable virtue‚Äîit evolves to become more virtuous. Note: This is our working hypothesis that we are prototyping and experimenting with at recursive.eco.

Our Working Hypothesis

Every action stems from someone trying to meet a universal human need‚Äîconnection, autonomy, safety, contribution, meaning. Even harmful actions are tragic attempts to meet real needs through strategies that don't work.

When needs conflict, we don't choose sides. We look for ways to honor the underlying needs of everyone involved. Sometimes this means creative solutions that meet everyone's needs. Sometimes it means acknowledging that certain strategies for meeting needs cause harm to others, and gently redirecting toward strategies that work.

Our Infrastructure Helps People:
  • ‚Ä¢ Recognize their own needs and the needs of others
  • ‚Ä¢ Express those needs without blame or demand
  • ‚Ä¢ Collaborate on strategies that meet multiple needs at once
  • ‚Ä¢ Modify our systems when they're not serving everyone's needs
When Strategies Cause Harm:
  • ‚Ä¢ First assume good intent and offer support
  • ‚Ä¢ Use clear communication to help translate between different expressions
  • ‚Ä¢ Provide clear feedback about strategies that support the commons
  • ‚Ä¢ Limit access when needed, while keeping the door open for return

This hypothesis itself can be modified by the community. If it's not serving everyone's needs, we'll evolve it together. The goal isn't perfection‚Äîit's learning. We're experimenting with infrastructure that helps humans cultivate virtue together, even when we disagree.
